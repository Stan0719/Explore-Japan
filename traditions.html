Traditions

Discover the timeless customs that shape Japanese life, offering a window into a society where respect and ritual are woven into everyday life.

Japanese Traditions
Category Description Key Points Image
Bowing and Formalities Bowing is a significant part of Japanese culture, used for greetings, gratitude, and showing respect.
  • Eshaku (15-degree): Greeting new people
  • Keirei (30-degree): Showing respect to superiors
  • Saikerei (45-degree): Highest form of respect
  • Use of titles like "san" and "sama"
Eating at Traditional Restaurants Dining customs emphasize politeness and appreciation for food.
  • Remove shoes and sit on tatami floors
  • Say "Itadakimasu" before eating and "Gochisosama deshita" after
  • Slurping indicates enjoyment
  • Polite drinking involves filling others' glasses first
Japanese Tea Ceremony (Chanoyu) A ritual art form representing harmony, respect, and Zen principles.
  • Originated from Chinese tea culture in the 9th century
  • Chado emphasizes Zen Buddhism and respect for nature
  • Formal ceremony lasts about 4 hours
  • Guests purify themselves before entering
